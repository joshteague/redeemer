2012 / Jan - Apr

A few couples in Marietta begin to sense God leading them to start a church in Marietta, according to God’s unconditional grace in Jesus.

2012 / May

One of the founding elders has lunch with a pastor from Atlanta to talk about a possible church plant in Marietta. The two families beginning the church trust in the belief that if God is making the details clear in their lives to start a church, then he will do the same in the life of a pastor to come and serve.

2012 / Jun - Sep

The two founding elders, founding pastor, and their wives meet every other week to discuss what a church might look like that preaches the sufficiency of Jesus’ work from every text of the Bible. During this time, and the subsequent two years, God worked through a handful of pastors and churches to support the foundation of Redeemer.

2013 / Jan

Twenty people begin gathering for prayer, worship, and Bible study on Thursday evenings in a dentist office. Over the next four months, while visitors come in and out of the Bible study, these individuals became the first parishioners of Redeemer. Simultaneously, Redeemer’s initial core families start meeting in a home on Sunday mornings for breakfast, worship, and a time of biblical teaching for their children. The simple acts of breaking bread, worshiping, and learning alongside our children facilitated a great sense of community. These Sunday mornings served as the organic model for Redeemer’s Family Worship service.

2013 / Apr

Redeemer holds its first Easter service at Henry Memorial Park.

2013 / Apr

Redeemer concludes the Thursday night Bible study but continues to meet on Sunday mornings in a home, adding an hour of teaching for the adults.

2013 / Sep

By God’s great provision and goodness, Redeemer launches Sunday morning services at the Walker School. Community Dinner Groups begin meeting monthly to allow people to get to know each other beyond Sunday mornings.

2013 / Dec

Redeemer holds its first Christmas Eve service at the The Brickyard in downtown Marietta.

2014 / Jan

Redeemer launches its first small group to help people know others, as well as be known by others in the context of studying and discussing scripture.

2015 / June

Redeemer purchases a dilapidated old knitting factory (531 Roselane Street) and hosts building tours and vision casting discussions for how the building may best serve the church and community.

2015 / November

Construction estimates exceed Redeemer’s cash on hand.

2015 / December

Redeemer’s elders decide to postpone construction and wait on the Lord. Three weeks later, enough money is given to begin construction.

2016 / January

Construction begins at 531 Roselane Street.

2016 / August

First Sunday morning service at 531 Roselane Street.

2016 / August

Redeemer Student Ministry attends their first retreat.

2017 / Summer - Fall

The next round of renovations begin at 531 Roselane Street – constructing a student ministry area, elementary classrooms, and several offices.

2018 / Winter

The final round of renovations begin at 531 Roselane Street – additional preschool classrooms, a permanent and larger sanctuary, and a rear entrance to the building.
